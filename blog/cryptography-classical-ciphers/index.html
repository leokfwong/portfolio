Cryptography - Classical Ciphers

10 min read
16 January 2020

When we think about cybersecurity, the terms DES, RSA, AES and so on come to mind. These are all common encryption algorithms used in today's digital world. However, there exists many other cryptography techniques that have been used in the past. If we go not too far back in time, we might've heard of ENIGMA, an encryption device used during World War II. Or perhaps we remember the Babington Plot and the Vigen√®re Cipher dating back to the 15th and 16th century. The Caesar Cipher is a well-known example from the Roman Age. Although all of these techniques have become obsolete with time, it could still be interesting to understand how they worked and how they were eventually broken. Without further ado, we will explore several classical cryptography concepts and mechanisms.

Monoalphabetic ciphers

Monoalphabetic ciphers are a type of cipher in which each alphabet character mapping is fixed and unique. For example, if "A" is encrypted as "Z", all letters "A" in the message are encrypted as "Z".
