About Me

I am a graduate student at University of Montreal pursuing a master’s degree in Computer Science. I have a strong background in data science with an expertise in data management and data processing. I also have experience in web development and I am interested in cybersecurity technologies.

Previously, I completed my Bachelor of Science Degree in Biology and Computer Science at McGill University (2015). Motivated to acquire a deeper understanding of the numerous topics I was interesting in, I pursued and earned a Diploma in Internet Business Technology (2017) and a Certificate in Data Science & Machine Learning (2018) from the same university.

Experience

I have over 5 years of working experience at various companies and institutions, most notably the McGill University Health Centre (MUHC). I was the Data Manager for the Canadian Co-Infection Cohort, a prospective clinical cohort of HIV and Co-Infected patients. In that role, I oversaw the development and implementation of efficient ways to extract, transform and analyze data with high attention to confidentiality and security.

Working in the field of research, I had many opportunities to collaborate with doctors and researchers, where I was able to contribute in the data manipulation and analysis phases of different projects. I have also worked as a consultant for other medical clinics and healthcare organizations, providing insight to their data related issues and helping them with optimization processes.

On the side, I have also done some freelance web development and design. I love teaching myself new things, there is just so much to learn! With how accessible knowledge is nowadays, there is just no excuse for not being curious :)

Cryptography - Classical Ciphers

10 min read
16 January 2020

When we talk about cybersecurity, the terms DES, RSA, AES and so on come to mind. In fact, these are all common encryption algorithms used in today's digital world. However, there exists many other techniques in the history of cryptography. Dating back from World War II, ENIGMA was an encryption device used for protected communication purposes. Going a bit further back in time, the Babington Plot and the Vigenère Cipher were ciphers used in the 15th and 16th century. Another classic example is the Caesar Cipher from the Roman Age. Although the majority of these techniques have become obsolete with time, it could still be interesting to understand how they worked and how they were eventually broken. We will explore several classical cryptography concepts and mechanisms, along with some concrete examples to illustrate their applications.
